Overview

Excel is a great tool for inputting and visualizing data; Especially when it comes to company financials and general book keeping. You can model all of the statements with minimal effort. There are some activities outlined below to help you build on your knowledge from the video.

Income Statement - Dean sells 150 lemonades in the month of July. The price of each lemonade sold during the month was $3. The variable cost per lemonade is $.50. He also has some fixed costs associated with this business. He paid $50 in wages to a friend of his, the renting of the stand for the month was $15, transportation costs totaled $75, and utilities totaled $15. Create an income statement for Dean's stand, what is his net profit for this particular month? What could he do to garner more profit? Check your statement against the one provided below.

Balance Sheet - Arthur's Tree Trimming has the following assets: $20,000 in cash, $15,000 in Accounts Receivable, $75,000 in Plant Property and Equipment, and another $10,000 in non-current assets. Arthur's business has the following liabilities: $5,000 in Accounts Payable, $2,500 in Notes Payable, and $60,000 in long-term debt. Arthur's business has the following equity: $30,000 in common stock and $22,500 in Retained Earnings from profits in preceding years. Create an organized balance sheet to record all of this information for our client Arthur.

Statement of Cash Flow - Sammy has the following cash flows from operation: Net earnings for the year were $880,000, Depreciation was $9,000, and decrease in Accounts Receivable was $30,000. There was some negative cash flow from investing: $100,000 went towards the purchase of new Plant, Property, and Equipment. There was a net positiive cash flow from financing as well: $15,000 in the form of Notes Payable. Create an organized cash flow statement to record all of this information for our client Sammy.
